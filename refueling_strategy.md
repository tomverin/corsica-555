# Corsica 555 2026 - Refueling Strategy

Generated from `555-gravel-poi.gpx`, `555_Corsica__2_2026_vfinale.gpx`,
and `terrain_analysis.md` on 2026-05-08.

Assumption: race carbohydrates are carried from the start as gels and drink
powder. External resupply is therefore mainly for:

- water;
- solid/salty food;
- caffeine/coffee;
- comfort food;
- emergency calories;
- toilets and short mental resets.

## Core Strategy

Carry the full carbohydrate plan from the start. Do not rely on shops for race
fuel. Use POIs to keep the body functional: water, salt, real food, stomach
comfort, and morale.

Working intake targets:

- Carbohydrates: ~60 g/h while riding from carried gels/powder. This is the
  deliberate target after gut trouble at 90 g/h on the 400 km BRM.
- Fluid: 500-750 ml/h in normal conditions, more if hot.
- Sodium/potassium: add diet salt mix to bottles, adjusted by heat and sweat
  rate. Keep sodium intake separate enough from carbohydrate that it can be
  increased without forcing more sugar.
- Solid food: small amounts every 3-5 h, mostly salty/bland.

### 34 h race-plan fuel load

Roadbook target is now ~34 h elapsed, but the carried carbohydrate plan should
cover **riding time**, not every elapsed hour. The practical target is:

- Engineered fuel carried from start: **~32 h × ~60 g/h = ~1,900 g carbs**.
- This covers ~30-31 h riding plus a small margin.
- Stops, sleep, and shop solids do not need full gel/powder intake.
- Shops remain useful for water, salt, texture, coffee/Coca-Cola, and morale,
  not as the primary carbohydrate source.

Simple race setup:

- **16 Nduranz gels** = 720 g carbs, ~7,360 mg sodium, ~1.25 kg carried.
- **32 powder doses** at ~37-40 g carbs per bottle = ~1,200-1,280 g carbs.
- Total engineered carbs: **~1,920-2,000 g**.
- Expected carried mass: **~2.5-2.6 kg** before small bags/diet salt.
- Practical packing: **8 blocks of 4 h**. Each block = 2 Nduranz gels + 4
  powder doses.

Hourly rhythm:

- One carbohydrate bottle per hour with ~37-40 g carbs.
- Half a Nduranz gel per hour on average, easier as **1 gel every 2 h**.
- One pure-water bottle always available.
- Add diet salt to the water bottle when heat/sweat demand it.
- Sodium baseline with this setup is roughly **450 mg/h** from powder + Nduranz,
  or **~730 mg/h** when adding 1.1 g diet salt to the water bottle each hour.

This is deliberately not a heavy backup plan. Real-food backup comes from
small salty items and course shops.

### Sodium / diet salt reference

Current diet salt composition:

- Sodium: **26.24 g / 100 g**.
- Potassium: **17.31 g / 100 g**.
- Salt equivalent: **65.6 g / 100 g**.

Practical dose:

- **1.1 g diet salt per bottle** = ~**289 mg sodium** + ~**190 mg potassium**
  (~0.72 g salt equivalent).
- This is useful but moderate. After a heavy heat-training sweat loss (~1.5 L),
  it helps recovery but should be paired with normal salty food and adequate
  rehydration.

Race implication:

- Baseline in hot conditions: add ~**1.1 g diet salt** to the water bottle when
  using the two-bottle setup (one pure water / one carbohydrate bottle), unless
  the stomach says otherwise.
- With the current planned nutrition (Nduranz every ~2 h + carbohydrate powder
  bottle), this likely brings sodium intake into the **~650-750 mg/h** range.
- In very hot conditions or if sweat rate is high, target **800-1 000 mg sodium/h**
  using extra sodium/diet salt without forcing extra carbohydrate.

Do not wait until hungry. Eat before rough sectors because eating on track or
technical descents will be harder.

### Sleep approach

The roadbook has two valid sleep modes:

- **Plan A**: a real 2-3 h sleep/reset around the deep-night section if it
  keeps the race clean.
- **Alternative B**: no scheduled sleep, only 10-45 min opportunistic rest if
  lucidity drops.

Do not force food during a real sleep stop. Eat before stopping, then restart
with water + a simple carbohydrate cue. The decision criterion is real-time
signal quality on the bike: handlebar drift, missed turns, or poor line choice
on easy terrain → stop briefly at the next safe spot. Otherwise, keep moving.

Caffeine is the active counter-measure for sleep pressure on this plan.
Reserve caffeine capsules for the **02:00-07:00 block** (Saturday, deepest
pressure window), not for the start. Coffee/Coca-Cola remain useful morale
tools, but the capsules are the controlled caffeine source.

## Timing Consequences

- km 377.8: Saturday late morning to midday.

This means the longest solid-food gap, km 182.2 to km 305.5, still matters. The
km 305.5 convenience store may or may not be usable depending on sleep mode and

Therefore:

- Use km 78, 164, and 182 as the last realistic daytime/evening solid-food
  opportunities.
- Carry enough non-sweet solid food from km 182 to survive until at least km
  377.8 if km 305.5 is closed or not useful.
- Treat shops as opportunities, not dependencies.

### Practical 34 h stop budget

A 34 h target allows one or two real resets, but not lots of drifting stops. A
workable budget:

- km 59.6: 5-8 min if needed.
- km 78.0: 10-15 min, main daytime solid stop.
- km 164/182: one useful stop plus one quick stop. Total 15-25 min.
- km 200.5: water only, 3-5 min.
- km 250.1: water only, 3-5 min.
- km 305.5 or CP2 area: 10-20 min if needed for water, food, or sleep reset.
- km 377.8-387.9: 10-20 min breakfast-style reset if useful.
- km 429/440: 5-10 min final technical-sector reset.

Total non-sleep planned stop time: ~2.5-3.5 h. Sleep can be 0-45 min in
Alternative B, or 2-3 h in Plan A.

### Late rough sectors under 34 h

Under the 34 h target, the late technical sectors should still be in daylight
if the race remains controlled:

- km 389-411: Saturday late morning / early afternoon.
- km 417-419: Saturday early afternoon.
- km 441-455: Saturday afternoon.

That is good. The risk is not darkness; it is sleep pressure, heat, shoulder
irritability, and degraded judgment. Keep the caffeine plan for the 02:00-07:00
block, not for the start.

## What To Carry From Start

Because all race carbohydrates are carried:

- Full gel/powder allocation for the race.
- Powder portioned into bottles/sachets by block.

## Useful Items Available On Course

When stopping at a bakery / convenience / café, prefer salty / cold /
familiar over sweet (gels already cover sweet). Reliable Corsica buys:

- Sandwich jambon-cru / jambon-fromage / paysan (bakeries).
- Pizza part / quiche / fougasse salée.
- Chips, cacahuètes salées, bretzels.
- Fromage emballé (Babybel, vache qui rit, comté tranché).
- Charcuterie locale (figatellu, lonzu) — lighter doses, gut sensitive.
- Coca-Cola 33 cl (caffeine + sugar + cold).
- Orangina / jus de pomme (variety + sugar without gel-overlap).
- Banane si dispo.
- Café espresso (cafés) — short caffeine pulse.
- Eau pétillante (variation pour aider digestion).

Avoid:

- Anything not tested in training.
- Heavy creamy items (mayo, sauces, salades composées).
- Local raw cheese / saucisson if gut already complaining.

## Notes For Stomach Management

- Use powder/gels for predictable carbohydrate intake.
- Use shops for texture and salt, not primary calories.
- Do not introduce exotic foods mid-race.
- If nausea starts: reduce intensity, sip water, switch briefly to bland/salty
  solid food, then resume carbs gradually.
- If sweetness fatigue starts: buy salty food at km 78, 164, 182, 305, 378-386,
  429-433, or 462+.

## Contingencies

### Bakery at km 378 closed
If the first option is closed, push to km 385.5 (second bakery + Epicerie du
Col). If both are closed, continue with carried food to the km 428.7-433.3
cluster. Keep one savoury item from the previous evening / km 182 stop in
jersey pocket as backup.

### km 305.5 convenience closed at night
Possible depending on Plan A/B timing. Treat as water/reset point only unless
obviously open. Real shops are more reliable from km 377.7 onward.

### Sequence C (km 440-455) takes longer than planned
Possible if shoulder/knee react badly or the surface is wet. Walk
subsections of the descent — it is a valid race tactic. Worst case adds
~30 min. The post-Sequence C urban density at km 462+ has many recovery
options.

### Sleep pressure earlier than expected
Take it at feel. Do not push past clear signal degradation (handlebar
drift, missed turns, bad line choice on easy terrain). Five minutes
eyes-closed at a fontaine costs nothing and may save the race. Caffeine
capsules are the first counter-measure; Coca-Cola is a secondary morale/sugar
option; sleep is the second.

### Heat (>26 °C)
Bump fluid to 1 L+/h and sodium to 800-1 000 mg/h. Add fontaine stops
to wet head/jersey on the climbs. Slow the pace if necessary — 34 h is a
target, not a constraint.

### Mechanical or crash
Race rules: any external mechanical assistance = exclusion. Plan
self-repair: tubeless plugs, tube, boot, multitool. In case of a crash
needing medical attention, you must restart from the exact location
after care. Notify PC course immediately.

## Critical Reminders

- Never leave a water point before a 40-50 km gap with low bottles.
- Eat before the rough sectors; rough terrain is not where you solve a calorie
  deficit.
- Late race priority is judgment and bike handling. Underfueling will show up
  as bad line choice before it shows up as leg failure.
- **60 % fontaine rule**: any time you pass a fontaine and any bottle is
  below 60 %, stop 30 seconds and refill. Always. The cost is nothing; the
  benefit on Corsica is significant.
- **Caffeine reserved for the 02:00-07:00 block**, not for the start. The
  morning sleep-pressure window is when the body actually needs it.
- **One decisive stop > five small ones** in the urban final zone (km 462+).
  The 70 POIs after Bastia are a temptation to fragment the finish — pick
  one if needed, then ride home.
